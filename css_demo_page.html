Hyderabad Biryani

Ingredients :

  • 500gm Chicken(cut into pieces)
  • 2 cup basmati rice
  • 2 Onions (sliced)
  • 2 Tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 cup curd
  • 2 tbsp Biryani Masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 4 Green Cardamons
  • 4 Cloves
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 Bay Leaves
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Ghee/Oil
  • salt to taste

Preparation :

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, biryani masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, salt, and half the ginger-garlic paste. Set aside for 1 hour.
  2. Cook basmati rice with whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves) until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat ghee/oil in a large pot, add onions, and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add the remaining ginger-garlic paste, tomatoes, and marinated chicken. Cook until chicken is tender.
  5. Layer the semi-cooked rice over the chicken. Garnish with fried onions, saffron, coriander, and mint leaves.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes (dum cooking).
  7. Serve hot with raita.

Paneer Bhurji

Ingredients :

  • 200g Paneer (crumbled)
  • 1 Onion ( finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • Green chilli
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Red chilli powder
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • fresh coriender leaves
  • salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp tumeric powder

Preparation :

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown
  3. Add tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  4. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well
  5. Add crumbled paneer and mix well
  6. Sprinkle garam masala and cook for another minute.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with roti, paratha, or bread.

Kadai Paneer

Ingredients :

  • 200g paneer (cut into cubes)
  • 1 Cup hung curd
  • 1 tsp Gram Flour (Besan)
  • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 1 tsp Red Chilli Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 Tumeric Powder
  • 1/2 tsp chaat masala
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • salt to taste
  • Skewers
  • Oil for brushing
  • Onion,Capsicum and tomato

Preparation :

  1. Prepare the marinade: In a bowl, mix yogurt, gram flour, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, chaat masala, lemon juice, and salt.
  2. Add paneer cubes (and vegetables if using) to the marinade. Mix well and marinate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Preheat the grill or oven to 200°C
  4. Capsicum and Onions should be cooked only for two minutes. It is tasty to get the crunchy pieces when you eat the Curry.
  5. If you add hot water to cook the vegetables, then the flavours of the Spices you add will not get diluted. If you add cold Water, the Spices lose their flavour.
  6. Paneer becomes soft if it is placed in hot water 10 minutes before it is added to the Curry. And it tastes better too.
  7. The Ghee that we add at the end gives a nice aroma to the Curry. If you don’t like Ghee, you can drop it.

Fish Curry

Ingredients :

  • 500g Fish Fillets(such as pomfret,tilapia or salmon)
  • 1 Onionm (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp Coconut Milk
  • 1 tbsp Ginger Garlic Paste
  • 1 tsp red Chilli Powder
  • 1/2 tsp tumeric powder
  • 1 tsp corinder powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2-3 curry leaves
  • salt to taste
  • Oil
  • Fresh Coriander (for garnish)

Preparation :

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and curry leaves, and let them splutter.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté until it turns translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute
  4. Add tomato puree, red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Cook until the oil starts to separate.
  5. Add coconut milk and salt, and simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add fish fillets and gently cook for 7-10 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot with steamed rice.
